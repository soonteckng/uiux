The Problem

University students aged 18-23 struggle with career exploration through traditional methods like job descriptions, career fairs, and generic advice. These approaches fail to provide immersive, real-world understanding of professions, leading to confusion, indecision, and suboptimal career choices. Current tools are often static with limited interactivity or personalized feedback, leaving students with incomplete or outdated information about actual day-to-day work responsibilities.

üí° Proposed Solution

A hybrid web-AR platform combining web browsing with Augmented Reality (AR) simulations. Students experience realistic job scenarios, complete gamified skill assessments with personalized feedback, and receive AI-driven career recommendations. The platform bridges the gap between theoretical job descriptions and actual workplace experiences, boosting decision-making confidence.

Design Challenges & Solutions

‚ö†Ô∏è AR Inexperience (55.6% of users)

Most students had never used AR before and felt uncertain about the technology despite interest.

‚úì Solution: Designed intuitive onboarding guides, progressive disclosure of AR features, and clear instructions for spatial interactions. Fallback: 3D video walkthrough alternative if AR not supported.

‚ö†Ô∏è Information Overload

Wireframe feedback showed assessment pages and career details felt dense and cognitively overwhelming.

‚úì Solution: Implemented card-based layouts, chunked content into sections (About, Key Skills, Interactive Options), simplified pages using recognition over recall (career imagery replacing text), and moved completed assessments to profile for focused navigation.

‚ö†Ô∏è Cross-Channel Navigation Clarity

Transitioning between desktop research and mobile AR simulations was not intuitive; users lost sense of progress when switching devices.

‚úì Solution: Added breadcrumb navigation, clear visual instructions for device handoffs (e.g., "Connect to your mobile"), synchronization markers showing "save states" across platforms, and progress indicators that update in real-time.

‚ö†Ô∏è Accessibility & Visual Design

Initial wireframes lacked consideration for text sizes, color contrast, and navigation alternatives.

‚úì Solution: Increased font sizes on mobile, improved color contrast ratios, ensured accessible touch targets, and provided alternative navigation paths. Enhanced visual hierarchy through icons and clear call-to-action buttons.

The Problem

Drivers frequently experience back pain, neck stiffness, leg cramps, and fatigue due to poor driving ergonomics and prolonged sitting. This gap between ergonomic vehicle design and actual driver behavior leads to accumulated discomfort over time, reduced driving safety, and long-term musculoskeletal health issues. Most drivers manage discomfort reactively, either through stretching, reclining seats, or cushions rather than proactively preventing it.

üí° Proposed Solution

An in-vehicle AI ergonomics assistant that continuously monitors posture and fatigue through seat sensors, steering sensors, and in-car cameras, then provides adaptive real-time feedback via the instrument cluster and infotainment system. The system guides posture correction, detects emerging fatigue, and encourages scheduled breaks during long drives.

Key Features

üéØ Real-Time Posture Monitoring

System continuously analyzes sitting posture and provides immediate corrective feedback via infotainment screen and audio cues when posture degrades

üò¥ Multi-Trigger Fatigue Detection

Combines eye movement tracking, posture changes, and driving behavior to detect fatigue before it becomes dangerous

üõë Smart Break Recommendations

Suggests breaks based on driving duration and fatigue indicators to minimize accumulated physical and mental strain

üéÆ Steering-Wheel Alert Control

Drivers can dismiss alerts using a steering-wheel button, keeping eyes on the road and reducing distraction

üìä Fatigue Level Visualization

Visual fatigue indicators (low/high) on instrument cluster show real-time fatigue progression, eliminating abrupt transitions

‚úÖ Posture Confirmation Feedback

System confirms when driver has successfully corrected posture, providing clear closure and actionable guidance

Design Challenges & Solutions

‚ö†Ô∏è System Visibility & Interpretability

Evaluators felt posture feedback seemed "artificial" and weren't sure what the system was sensing or how corrections were validated.

‚úì Solution: Added clear information prompts on the instrument cluster displaying "Eyes tracked", "Back detected", "Hands on Wheel?" and confirmation message "Posture fixed" to communicate system status.

‚ö†Ô∏è Alert Dismissal & Driver Safety

Cancelling alerts via the touchscreen was distracting and unsafe, requiring eyes off the road during critical driving moments.

‚úì Solution: Implemented a steering-wheel button for quick alert dismissal, keeping driver focus on the road. This was highly valued by evaluator Vanessa for reducing distraction.

‚ö†Ô∏è Abrupt Fatigue Transitions

The jump from normal to "Fatigued" state felt sudden and unclear‚Äîusers couldn't see gradual fatigue buildup or understand the threshold.

‚úì Solution: Introduced real-time fatigue level indicators (e.g., an eye icon that changes color from green to yellow to red) showing progressive fatigue accumulation, making transitions interpretable.

‚ö†Ô∏è Font Visibility for Fatigued Drivers

Small text on instrument cluster was difficult to read, especially when drivers were already cognitively fatigued (feedback from Ryan Ng and Daniel Wong).

‚úì Solution: Increased font sizes for driving-time indicators and enlarged fatigue-eye icon to ensure visibility during all driving conditions.

Safety-Critical Design Approach

‚ö†Ô∏è Core Safety Principles

  • Minimal Cognitive Load: Alerts are non-intrusive and auto-dismiss after 1 minute if not acknowledged
  • Hands-On-Wheel Controls: Steering wheel button for alert dismissal ensures eyes stay on road
  • Clear System State: Real-time indicators show exactly what the system is monitoring and why
  • Passive Automatic Operation: System requires no manual setup or configuration, it starts automatically when vehicle ignition begins
