SkillScape: Immersive Career Exploration Platform

Project Overview

SkillScape addresses a critical gap in career exploration for university students aged 18-23. Traditional career discovery methods (job descriptions, career fairs, generic advice) fail to provide immersive, real-world understanding of professions. This hybrid web-AR platform combines browsing with Augmented Reality simulations, offering dynamic and engaging career experiences.

Sketched Paper Prototype

Design Process

Research Phase: Conducted user research with 18 survey respondents and qualitative testing to understand career exploration habits and expectations. Students prioritized job stability (77.8%), work-life balance (77.8%), and growth opportunities (77.8%).

Ideation: Created personas, scenarios, and cross-channel journey maps. Developed information architecture with two main task flows: completing career AR simulations and taking skill assessments.

Prototyping: Built high-fidelity prototype in Figma with focus on simplicity, cross-device consistency, and emotional engagement through gamification.

Testing & Iteration: Usability tested with 2 final-year university students. Refined information architecture based on cognitive load principles and cross-channel logic.

Prototype Interface

Key Features

  • Day in the Life AR Simulations: Interactive job scenarios allowing students to experience realistic work tasks
  • Gamified Skill Assessments: Quizzes and challenges with progress tracking and badges
  • AI-Driven Recommendations: Personalized career suggestions based on assessment results and user preferences
  • Cross-Platform Sync: Seamless progress synchronization between desktop and mobile devices

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1 - AR Inexperience: 55.6% of users had never used AR. Solution: Implemented intuitive onboarding guides and progressive disclosure to support new-to-AR users without overwhelming complexity.

Challenge 2 - Information Overload: Complex assessment layouts confused users. Solution: Restructured pages with chunked content, card-based layouts, and simplified navigation to reduce cognitive load.

Challenge 3 - Cross-Channel Clarity: Transitions between devices lacked clarity. Solution: Added visual cues, clear save states, and synchronized progress indicators across platforms.

Results & User Feedback

Usability testing revealed high satisfaction scores (4.5/5) with low perceived difficulty (1.5/5). Users appreciated the intuitive AR interface and personalized recommendations. The iterative design process resulted in a more accessible, engaging platform aligned with real student career exploration needs.

My Role

As part of a 6-person team (UX 4-2), I contributed to the design process including prototype development and visual design implementation. Collaborated on information architecture refinement and usability testing coordination.

Driven Well: AI Driver Ergonomics & Fatigue Monitoring System

Problem Statement

Drivers experience chronic discomfort and pain (back pain, neck stiffness, leg cramps) due to poor driving ergonomics and prolonged sitting, leading to reduced productivity, health issues, and increased accident risk. While modern vehicles have ergonomic features, drivers often fail to adjust them properly or maintain correct posture during long drives.

Sketched Paper Prototype

Design Process

Research Phase: Conducted contextual inquiry and interviews with 4 participants (students, lecturers, delivery drivers) experiencing varying levels of driving-related discomfort. Identified gaps between ergonomic vehicle design and actual user behavior.

Task Analysis: Mapped 6 key user tasks: manual posture adjustment, convenient automated adjustment, real-time posture feedback, personalized reporting, fatigue monitoring, and smart break recommendations.

Design Ideation: Created 3 design sketches and selected the AI-Integrated Smart Dashboard Design focusing on real-time posture feedback and fatigue detection—the features with highest impact.

Prototyping & Testing: Built paper and digital prototypes. Conducted heuristic evaluation and usability testing with domain experts and target users.

Prototype Interface

Key Features

  • Automatic Posture Detection: AI-powered camera and seat sensors continuously monitor driving posture
  • Real-Time Posture Feedback: Visual and audio alerts guide users to correct posture degradation before pain develops
  • Fatigue Detection: Multi-indicator system analyzing eye movement, facial features, and steering behavior
  • Smart Break Recommendations: Suggests optimal rest intervals based on trip duration and accumulated ergonomic strain
  • Intuitive Alert Dismissal: Steering wheel button for safe, distraction-free alert management

Design Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1 - System Visibility: Users couldn't tell what the system was sensing. Solution: Added information prompts on instrument cluster explaining system activation, posture status, and confirmation messages when posture is corrected.

Challenge 2 - Alert Management During Driving: Requiring infotainment screen interaction was distracting and unsafe. Solution: Implemented steering wheel button for safe, quick alert dismissal without removing hands from wheel.

Challenge 3 - Fatigue Threshold Clarity: Abrupt transition from normal to fatigued state felt arbitrary. Solution: Introduced fatigue level indicators (Low/High) showing gradual buildup to increase interpretability.

Challenge 4 - Small Font Visibility: Fatigued drivers couldn't read small text on dashboard. Solution: Increased font sizes and enlarged eye icon, ensuring critical safety information remains visible.

Results & Validation

Final usability testing revealed strong positive feedback for automatic posture detection and steering wheel alert dismissal. Participants appreciated the system's minimal setup requirements and clear, continuous communication. Iterative testing across multiple rounds ensured design changes directly addressed real user needs in safety-critical driving contexts.

My Role

As part of a 6-person team (HCI 3-6), I actively contributed to user research through contextual inquiry, design artifact creation, prototype development, and usability testing facilitation. Involved in conducting heuristic evaluations and implementing design refinements based on participant feedback.
